- Adaptiveness (self-learning capability) is not mandatory for classifying an IT system as an AI under Article 3(1) AI Act.

- Recitals 12 and 128 clarify adaptiveness as changes occurring post-deployment due to self-learning.

- Identifying adaptiveness is important, as adaptive AI systems need more frequent risk assessments.

- Adaptiveness differs from autonomy: adaptiveness involves learning, whereas autonomy means independence from human control.

- Commission Guidelines on the definition of an artificial intelligence system, sections 22 and 23.
